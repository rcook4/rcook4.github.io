Opening Trigrams Subjects Technologies Essay

A n-gram is n-length series of terms from a text source.

Google Books Ngram Viewer is a frontend query tool to their growing corpus of n-grams from millions of scanned books in multiple languages across several centuries.

I wanted to determine if that corpus could be used to get a sense of what people were thinking about, by analyzing what people had written about.

Unfortunately, the entire dataset is multiple petabytes in size and therefore too large to download or host online.

Fortunately, Google does publicly host backend access to a static subset (English, 3-grams, 1520 to 2008) of the data as a BigQuery sample table trigrams.

I decided to use that data knowing I would be giving up some flexibility and therefore accuracy, for the sake of ease and usability.

The first step was to look for anomalies in the top trigrams.

The second step was to look for anomalies in the high frequency subjects.

The final step was to look for anomalies in a specific group of words, in this case technologies.
